# Clarkware Short Deck

## Slide 1: Clarkware

Facility-aware operational software for industrial work.

Integrated Production Environments combine job context, machine-linked evidence, messaging, and auditable human-and-AI coordination.

## Slide 2: Problem

Industrial work is still fragmented across tools, notes, exports, chat, and reconstructed reporting.

That creates:

- slower problem-solving
- weaker traceability
- lower trust
- knowledge loss

## Slide 3: Product

Clarkware is CLARK's software family for real production environments.

Version 1 starts with workstation-level Integrated Production Environments that unify:

- job context
- notes
- test and diagnostics artifacts
- job-linked messaging
- auditable AI support

## Slide 4: Wedge

Clarkware begins narrowly:

- one workstation IPE
- one evidence-import path
- one messaging model
- one AI support loop
- one facility reporting view

This is a believable wedge, not a whole-factory replacement claim.

## Slide 5: Why It Wins

If Clarkware becomes the place where jobs, notes, evidence, and AI actions are linked, it creates:

- stronger process memory
- better operator and manager visibility
- auditable coordination
- repeatable node software standards
- stronger licensing and governance leverage for CLARK

## Slide 6: Why Now

The enabling conditions now exist:

- Theia for modular workspaces
- XMPP for messaging and presence
- connected-worker demand
- bounded industrial AI adoption
- stronger need for traceability and accountability

## Slide 7: Niagara Pilot

First proof point:

- one diagnostics or test workstation
- one live evidence path
- one remote expert flow
- one AI summarizer or router
- one manager-facing reporting view

Success means real jobs move through the system with live evidence, live messaging, and live reporting.

## Slide 8: Closing

Clarkware does not need to replace the whole factory stack.

It only needs to prove a narrow workstation wedge that becomes the memory and coordination layer of the node.
