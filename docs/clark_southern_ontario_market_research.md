**CLARK**

Southern Ontario Electronics Market — Prospect Intelligence

Prepared for internal use. March 2025.

Scope: Electronics manufacturers and assembly centres in the Southern Ontario corridor relevant to Clark's training, node, and platform strategy.

**Purpose & Methodology**

This document maps electronics manufacturers and contract assembly operations across Southern Ontario that represent early outreach priorities for Clark. Companies are organized by geographic proximity to the Hamilton anchor node and assessed across three strategic lenses: IPC training pipeline opportunity, node candidate potential, and Clarkware software feedback value.

For each company we have documented publicly available information on certifications, services, market focus, and recent strategic activity, followed by a Clark-specific commentary on the most productive angle of engagement.

**Tier 1 — Golden Horseshoe / Immediate Corridor**

These companies sit closest to Hamilton and the Hamilton–Buffalo operating corridor. They represent the highest-priority outreach for market validation, training pipeline conversations, and potential node or partner development.

**Recommended Outreach Framework**

**By Engagement Type**

**IPC Training Pipeline Conversations**

Lead with: 'How does your team currently maintain IPC certification, and how do you handle recertification cycles?' These companies are direct early customers for Clark's Hamilton training facility:

-   Cygnus Electronics — already IPC-certified workforce, needs recertification cadence

-   CE3 Electronics — AS9100D and IPC certified, high standard, active quality culture

-   ECI Technology Group — defense and aerospace work demands Class 2/3 certification

-   Artaflex — multi-node, multi-cert operation with recurring workforce training needs

-   MIS Electronics — ISO dual-certified, growing workforce

**Node Candidate Screening**

Lead with: 'What does capacity and workflow look like for you on complex low-volume programs?' These operators fit Clark's independent node profile:

-   Brantco Technologies — proximate to Hamilton, smaller independent, limited public visibility

-   Permatech Electronics — mid-size independent, cost-competitive positioning

-   STIM Canada — low-to-medium volume, high-mix, strong process maturity

**Clarkware Software Feedback**

Lead with: 'How do you currently handle job traceability, firmware loading, and inspection records?' These operations have the technical depth to give meaningful product feedback:

-   CE3 Electronics — documentation culture already in place

-   Cygnus — test fixture design expertise, sophisticated manufacturing ops

-   Artaflex — multi-node operation running the exact coordination problems Clarkware solves

**Competitive Reference (Monitor, Don't Approach Yet)**

-   Celestica — benchmark for talent market and certification norms

-   Artaflex — primary competitive reference for the binational node model
